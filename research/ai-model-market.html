The AI Model Market: A Three-Party Pricing Game—Open-Source Strategy and Free-Riding in a Platform Ecosystem

Abstract

This paper develops a three-party pricing game model featuring a proprietary provider, an open-source provider, and an aggregator to analyze the pricing incentives of the open-source provider in the presence of free-riding by the aggregator. The model's key feature is twofold. First, the official open-source channel provides a service-quality premium relative to the aggregator channel, reflecting reliability, compliance, official support, documentation quality, enterprise integration, and update speed. Second, the open-source provider, in addition to revenue from its official channel, derives an increasing and convex brand benefit from its total ecosystem share. This endogenizes indirect monetization mechanisms such as enterprise customization and ecosystem expansion. The baseline model derives general pricing conditions under this channel-differentiated structure and shows that brand benefits reduce the open-source provider's optimal markup. Price stratification, pP* > pO* > pA*, is obtained as a parameter-region implication under a sufficient official-channel premium condition and the existing quality and cost orderings, rather than being imposed as a primitive assumption. Although the aggregator does not pay a direct licensing fee to the open-source provider, its channel expansion can still create a symbiotic relationship by increasing the open-source provider's total market share. Extensions further discuss versioning, multi-provider competition, dynamic pricing, multi-model aggregation, and welfare and policy implications; however, these results rely on additional assumptions about demand structure or entry conditions.
